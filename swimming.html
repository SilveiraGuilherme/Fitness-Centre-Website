Why choose swimming?

Swimming is a complete sport for both body and mind. Practicing this sport offers several benefits and, as it is a low-impact sport, it is recommended for everyone who wants to practice physical activity.

When swimming, we exercise our body's muscles all at the same time, which helps with strengthening, improves respiratory condition and helps burn calories. Another positive point of this physical activity is that it can be practiced from a baby to older ages.

If you haven't tried this pleasurable sport yet, perhaps now is the ideal time to go all out in this aquatic world.

Types of Strokes

Freestyle

Fast and efficient type of stroke, performed by alternating arms and breathing laterally. predominant swimming style among practitioners.

Backstroke

Swimming is performed on the back also with alternating movement of the arms, but with circular movements. Breathing is more easily controlled, as the face is above the water level.

Breaststroke

Swimming is performed with concomitant movement of the arms, performing a circle or "half moon" while the legs perform a "frog kick" movement. Breathing can be better controlled as the head is often above water level.

Butterfly

Swimming is performed with concomitant movement of the arms forward and backward, while the legs perform a "dolphin kick" movement. It requires a lot of coordination and endurance to perform.

Age Group Division

Baby Swimming

6 months to 3 years: Generally accompanied by a responsible adult, in this age group, they learn the basics of floating and develop some skills such as confidence.

Small Children

3 to 5 years:In this age group, the ability to float and also the correct movement of arms and legs begin to improve. They also enhance their motor coordination, body awareness, etc.

Children

6 to 13 years: In this age group, they learn the types of strokes. Classes generally focused at perfecting each swim and improving physical conditioning.

Adults

14 or more: In this age group, classes are focused on gradual learning and the practice of swimming as a sport that, in addition to physical health, benefits social and mental health. It may also include training for competitions.

Elderly Adults

50 or more: In this age group, Classes more focused on low-impact exercises for the cardiovascular system and also for the muscles, with a more therapeutic purpose.

* This division may vary according to the abilities and needs of each student.
